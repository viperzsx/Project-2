LEDs vs Halogens: LEDs are brighter, more energy-efficient, and last much longer than halogens. While halogens are cheaper upfront and easy to replace, LEDs save more money in the long run with better performance.

  • Brightness: LEDs offer white, focused beams; halogens emit yellowish light.
  • Lifespan: LEDs last thousands of hours; halogens burn out sooner.
  • Heat: LEDs stay cool; halogens run hot.
  • Cost: Halogens are budget-friendly; LEDs are a smart investment.

To upgrade your headlights or interior lighting, you first need to find your bulb size. Here’s how:

  1. Check the Owner’s Manual: Look under “Lighting” or “Bulb Replacement.”
  2. Look at the Existing Bulb: You’ll usually see a code like H11 or 9005 on it.
  3. Use Online Lookup Tools: Many parts websites let you search by year, make, and model.

Tip: Common sizes include H11, 9006, H4, and 7443.

Installing LED bulbs is usually easy, but here are a few things to know:

  • Polarity: If the bulb doesn’t light up, flip the connector 180°.
  • CANBus Issues: Some vehicles need resistors to avoid flickering or error messages.
  • Beam Pattern: Align LEDs so the chips face 3 and 9 o’clock for best visibility.
  • Secure Wires: Make sure any extra wiring or drivers are tucked away and sealed.

Test everything before driving—good alignment avoids blinding others and improves your night vision.
